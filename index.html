Narendra Modi

17 September 1950

Prime Minister of India

Narendra Modi is the 14th Prime Minister of India and has been in office since 2014. He is a leader who has made significant contributions to the development and progress of India. Prime Minister Modi has implemented a number of economic reforms, such as Make in India and Digital India, which have attracted foreign investment and created new job opportunities. He has also focused on improving the country's infrastructure and transportation systems, with the construction of new roads, airports, and ports. In addition, Prime Minister Modi has made efforts to address social issues, such as sanitation and financial inclusion, through initiatives like Swachh Bharat Abhiyan and Jan Dhan Yojana. Overall, Prime Minister Modi's leadership and dedication to the people of India have played a crucial role in the country's development and progress.

Biographies

  • Narendra Modi was born on September 17, 1950 in Vadnagar, Gujarat, India.
  • Modi received a degree in political science from Gujarat University in Ahmedabad.
  • He worked as a full-time worker for the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), a Hindu nationalist organization, and later joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).
  • Modi rose through the ranks of the BJP and was elected Chief Minister of Gujarat in 2001. He served in this role for three consecutive terms.
  • In 2014, Modi was elected Prime Minister of India and has held this position since. During his time in office, he has implemented various economic and social policies, including the launch of the Goods and Services Tax and the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan cleanliness campaign.
